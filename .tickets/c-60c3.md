# Fix: compose worker should trust planner's explicit count in purpose, not contradict it (WS-UT24)

**Bug**: For WS-UT24 (ses_23020c7c6ffeRjq86GDTEZ0Tl9), the planner's compose purpose was "List the user's 6 unread emails". Compose worker produced "No unread emails are available." Compose worker's interpretation of the projected `read` field overrode the explicit count in the planner's purpose.

**Theory**: Compose worker treats record fields as authoritative even when they appear to contradict an explicit count in the planner's purpose. There's also a possible secondary bug — `read:true` projection on emails returned by get_unread_emails may be semantically inverted.

**Suggested approach**:
1. Inspect display projection for the email record's `read` field — is `true` correctly meaning "read" or is the projection inverted for the unread surface?
2. Compose-worker prompt: "If the planner's purpose explicitly states a count or list of items to render, render those items. Do not conclude 'none available' when the planner has explicitly directed you to list N items."
3. General rule — applies to any compose case where field values look contradictory.
