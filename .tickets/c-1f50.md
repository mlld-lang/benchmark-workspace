# Migrate the cleanly-substitutable @tool* helpers to .mx accessors

Replace the subset of rig's `@tool*` reflection helpers whose behavior is a 1:1 match for the `.mx.*` accessors that ship in mlld (`createToolEntryMxView` in `interpreter/utils/field-access.ts:447`). Rewrite of the original ticket — see notes — to reflect what actually migrates and what doesn't.

## Scope: clean substitutions only

These eight helpers are drop-in. Their bodies just unwrap a wrapper shape and read a single field that the compiled `ToolInputSchema` exposes verbatim:

- `@toolUpdateArgs(@t)` -> `@t.mx.updateArgs`
- `@toolExactArgs(@t)` -> `@t.mx.exactArgs`
- `@toolAllowlist(@t)` -> `@t.mx.allowlist`
- `@toolBlocklist(@t)` -> `@t.mx.blocklist`
- `@toolOptionalBenign(@t)` -> `@t.mx.optionalBenignFields`
- `@toolDescription(@t)` -> `@t.mx.description`
- `@toolInstructions(@t)` -> `@t.mx.instructions`
- `@toolLabels(@t)` -> `@t.mx.labels` (verify the executable-fallback branch is unreachable in current rig usage; if not, keep the helper)

Estimated ~30-50 of the 174 original sites. Mechanical replace, low regression risk.

## Out of scope (and why)

The original ticket listed eleven helpers as drop-ins. Five of them are not — they encode rig-domain semantics on top of the compiled schema:

- `@toolControlArgs` filters out rig's `unconstrainedArgs` and merges read-tool declared param names; `.mx.controlArgs` does neither.
- `@toolPayloadArgs` adds `unconstrainedArgs` to the data fields; `.mx.dataArgs` doesn't.
- `@toolCanAuthorizeRoles` normalizes `false`/string/array/missing into a roles array (with input-record-aware defaulting); `.mx.canAuthorize` returns the raw `false | string | string[]`.
- `@toolCorrelateControlArgs` has a multi-control-arg heuristic when `correlate` is unset; `.mx.correlate` defaults to `false`.
- `@toolInputRecord` returns the user-authored `inputs:` record (consumers read its `.mx.entries`); `.mx.inputSchema` returns the compiled `ToolInputSchema` struct — different shape, not interchangeable.

Don't migrate these in this ticket. Either leave the helpers as-is, or shrink them to thin wrappers around the corresponding `.mx.*` accessor in a separate, smaller follow-up. Moving the rig-domain semantics into mlld would be a different design decision that needs its own discussion (e.g. should `unconstrainedArgs` become a tool-catalog field?).

Also out of scope:

- `@pairsToObject`/`@toolCatalogObject`/`@toolEntryObject` — these exist for an imported-collection wrapper-shape quirk (see comments in `tooling.mld:105-128`). Deleting them needs a separate spike to verify the wrapper issue is gone — assume nothing.
- The catalog-routing / phase-filtering / doc-rendering surface (`@phaseToolDocs`, `@renderToolDoc`, `@phaseCatalog`, `@routeToolCatalog`, `@synthesizedOperations`, `@synthesizedAuthorizations`, `@toolKind`, `@wrongPhaseToolFailure`, etc.) — not reflection, not replaced by `.mx`. Most of `tooling.mld`'s 699 lines stay.

## Honest size

`tooling.mld` is **699 lines**, not "23k". The reflection helpers covered by `.mx` occupy roughly lines 48-309 (~260 lines). After this ticket: ~150 lines deleted (~21% of the file), plus the eight helper definitions and re-exports. The other ~110 lines in that range belong to helpers staying as-is or shrinking to thin wrappers in a follow-up.

## Why this is worth doing

Cleanup hygiene. The eight helpers became redundant once mlld's input-schema primitive landed. Removing them means rig and the runtime read the same compiled struct for the fields they cover. No bench-utility ticket is blocked on this; it doesn't unlock anything in `tk ready`.

## What this is NOT

- Not a performance fix. Helper invocation is sub-millisecond and bench wall is LLM-bound (1-5s/turn). Heap-walk costs in tooling.mld:105-128 are about eager catalog materialization, orthogonal to leaf reads.
- Not a complete elimination of rig's reflection layer. ~5 helpers stay because they encode rig-domain logic, not primitive gaps.
- Not a "single source of truth" for everything tool-related — only for the schema fields that live in `ToolInputSchema`. `unconstrainedArgs` and friends are still rig-side.
- Not a blocker for c-775b (`@wrapTools`). c-775b's body says the dependency is preference, not technical.
