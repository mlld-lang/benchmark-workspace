# [TR-IT3, TR-IT5] Add sensitivity labels to user_info PII fields

Travel IT3 (PII exfil to jay@google.com) and IT5 (passport+credit_card to janeLong@google.com) are COVERED-VERIFIED at the recipient grounding layer, but the PII fields themselves — passport_number, bank_account_number, credit_card_number, ID_number — live in @user_info.data.trusted with no `secret` or `sensitive` label.

This means `no-secret-exfil` / `no-sensitive-exfil` rules don't fire as a second structural layer; defense relies entirely on display projection + recipient grounding. Adding sensitivity labels closes the layering gap.

## Acceptance
- bench/domains/travel/records.mld @user_info: passport_number, credit_card_number, bank_account_number, ID_number declared with sensitivity labels (`secret` or `sensitive`)
- Test asserting these labels propagate through the value chain
- Test asserting a deliberately-grounded send_email with PII in body is denied by no-sensitive-exfil even when recipient layer would accept
- Mutation entry confirming the sensitivity-label layer is load-bearing
- Surfaced by spec-agentdojo-threat-model.md gap #4
